🎯 GATE DA Exam Overview

📝

Paper Pattern

65 questions totalling 100 marks. Duration: 3 hours. Mix of MCQ (Multiple Choice) and NAT (Numerical Answer Type).

📊

AI Weightage

Artificial Intelligence carries approximately 15–20 marks out of 100 in the GATE DA paper.

🏗️

Other Sections

Probability & Statistics, Linear Algebra, Calculus, Programming & DSA, DBMS, Machine Learning.

⚠️

Negative Marking

MCQs: ⅓ negative marking for wrong answers. NATs: No negative marking. No partial marking.

📅

Exam Month

Usually held in February each year. Registration opens around August–September of the previous year.

🎓

Eligibility

Final year B.Tech/B.E., M.Sc., MCA, or equivalent. No age limit for appearing in GATE.

📖 Recommended Study Order

Follow this sequence for the most effective GATE DA AI preparation. Each step builds on knowledge from the previous ones.

  1. Ch 00 — Introduction to AI
    Start here: understand what AI is, its history, and the GATE DA exam structure. Sets the context for everything that follows.
  2. Ch 01 — Intelligent Agents
    Learn the foundational framework — agents, environments, rationality, and PEAS. This vocabulary is used throughout the course.
  3. Ch 02 — Uninformed Search
    Core search algorithms (BFS, DFS, UCS). High GATE weightage. Focus on time/space complexity and when to use each algorithm.
  4. Ch 03 — Informed Search
    Heuristic-based search including A*. Very frequently asked in GATE. Master admissibility/consistency proofs and A* trace problems.
  5. Ch 04 — Adversarial Search
    Game trees, minimax, alpha-beta pruning. Common GATE problem type. Practice computing minimax values on game trees.
  6. Ch 05 — Propositional Logic
    Logical foundations: connectives, truth tables, CNF/DNF, resolution. Directly tested in GATE. Practice resolution proofs.
  7. Ch 06 — Predicate Logic
    First-order logic, quantifiers, unification, skolemization. Builds on propositional logic. Focus on unification and resolution in FOL.
  8. Ch 07 — Reasoning Under Uncertainty
    Bayesian networks, inference, HMMs. Highest GATE weightage topic. Master joint probability calculations and d-separation.
  9. Ch 14 — Constraint Satisfaction
    CSPs, backtracking, arc consistency. Occasionally tested. Light but important for complete coverage.
  10. Ch 13 — Planning
    STRIPS, partial-order planning, GraphPlan. Sometimes tested in GATE. Understand STRIPS representation well.
  11. Ch 08 — ML Foundations
    Bias-variance, cross-validation, metrics. Bridges classical AI and modern ML. Essential background for neural networks.
  12. Ch 09 — Neural Networks & Deep Learning
    Perceptrons to transformers. Focus on backpropagation calculations and understanding architecture trade-offs.
  13. Ch 12 — Reinforcement Learning
    MDPs, value/policy iteration, Q-learning. Occasionally tested. Focus on Bellman equations and convergence.
  14. Ch 10 — Natural Language Processing
    Text processing, embeddings, language models. Low direct GATE weightage but good for conceptual understanding.
  15. Ch 11 — Computer Vision
    Image processing and CNN architectures. Low GATE weightage. Cover basics and move on.
  16. Ch 15 — Advanced Topics
    XAI, ethics, emerging areas. Rarely directly tested but good for holistic understanding and interviews.
  17. Ch 16 — GATE DA Preparation
    Exam strategy, PYQs, formula sheet, revision. Do this last — consolidate everything with targeted practice.
