GATE Previous Year Questions: Adversarial Search

GATE Relevance: High

Question 1 GATE CS 2004

Minimax Value Calculation

Q: Consider the following game tree. MAX moves first. What value does the root node get using the minimax algorithm?

           MAX
          / | \
       MIN MIN MIN
       /\   /\   /\
      5  3  8  2  4  6

Options: (A) 3   (B) 4   (C) 5   (D) 6

Solution

Step 1 — Evaluate MIN nodes:

  • Left MIN = min(5, 3) = 3
  • Middle MIN = min(8, 2) = 2
  • Right MIN = min(4, 6) = 4

Step 2 — Evaluate MAX root: max(3, 2, 4) = 4

Answer: (B) 4

Question 2 GATE CS 2011

Alpha-Beta Pruning: Counting Pruned Nodes

Q: In the following game tree, how many leaf nodes will be pruned by alpha-beta pruning (left-to-right traversal)?

              MAX
            /      \
         MIN        MIN
        / \  \     / \  \
       5  3  9   2  8  1

Options: (A) 0   (B) 1   (C) 2   (D) 3

Solution

Traverse left-to-right:
Left MIN: Visit 5, then 3 → min so far = 3. Visit 9 → min = 3.
  No pruning here (all children visited).
  Back at MAX: α = 3

Right MIN: Visit 2 → v = 2.
  v = 2 ≤ α = 3 → α cutoff! Prune leaves 8 and 1.
  
Pruned nodes: 8, 1
Back at MAX: v = max(3, 2) = 3.

Answer: (C) 2

Question 3 GATE CS 2015

Complexity of Alpha-Beta

Q: The best-case time complexity of alpha-beta pruning on a game tree with branching factor b and depth d is:

Options: (A) O(bd)   (B) O(bd/2)   (C) O(b2d)   (D) O(d · b)

Solution

With perfect move ordering (best moves examined first at every node), alpha-beta pruning achieves optimal pruning. The number of leaf nodes evaluated reduces from bd to approximately bd/2.

Intuition: At MAX nodes, after finding the best move, subsequent children at sibling MIN nodes can be pruned. This effectively halves the exponent.

Answer: (B) O(bd/2)

Question 4 GATE DA 2023

Expectiminimax with Chance Nodes

Q: In the following game tree with chance nodes (coin flip: H with P=0.5, T with P=0.5), what is the value at the root? MAX moves first.

              MAX
            /      \
        CHANCE     CHANCE
        /   \       /   \
     (H)    (T)  (H)    (T)
    P=0.5  P=0.5 P=0.5  P=0.5
      |      |     |      |
     MIN    MIN   MIN    MIN
     /\     /\    /\     /\
    4  8   6  2  3  9   7  1

Solution

Step 1 — MIN nodes:
  MIN-1 = min(4,8) = 4    MIN-2 = min(6,2) = 2
  MIN-3 = min(3,9) = 3    MIN-4 = min(7,1) = 1

Step 2 — CHANCE nodes:
  Left CHANCE  = 0.5×4 + 0.5×2 = 2 + 1 = 3.0
  Right CHANCE = 0.5×3 + 0.5×1 = 1.5 + 0.5 = 2.0

Step 3 — MAX root:
  max(3.0, 2.0) = 3.0

Answer: 3.0

Question 5 GATE CS 2019

Optimal Strategy from Minimax

Q: Consider the following game tree. What is the optimal move for MAX at the root, and what is the guaranteed payoff?

              MAX (root)
           /     |     \
       MIN(A)  MIN(B)  MIN(C)
       / \      / \      / \
      7   2    5   8    9   1

Options:

(A) Move to A, payoff 2   (B) Move to B, payoff 5   (C) Move to C, payoff 1   (D) Move to A, payoff 7

Solution

  • MIN(A) = min(7, 2) = 2
  • MIN(B) = min(5, 8) = 5
  • MIN(C) = min(9, 1) = 1

MAX chooses: max(2, 5, 1) = 5 → Move to B.

Answer: (B) Move to B, payoff 5

Note: MAX cannot assume the opponent will make mistakes. Even though C contains the highest leaf (9), MIN at C would choose 1.

Question 6 GATE CS 2017

Alpha-Beta: Identify All Pruned Subtrees

Q: Apply alpha-beta pruning (left-to-right) to the following tree. Which leaf values are NOT evaluated?

                   MAX (root)
                /              \
           MIN(L)              MIN(R)
          /      \            /      \
       MAX(LL) MAX(LR)    MAX(RL) MAX(RR)
       / \      / \        / \      / \
      3   5    2   9      8   1    7   4

Solution

Traverse left-to-right:
MAX(LL): visit 3, then 5 → value = 5
MIN(L): v = 5, β = 5

MAX(LR): visit 2 → v = 2, α = 2
  visit 9 → v = 9, α = 9
  9 ≥ β(5)? Yes! β cutoff.
  But wait — 9 is already visited. LR returns 9.
  
MIN(L) = min(5, 9) = 5. Returns 5.
MAX(root): α = 5.

MIN(R): α from parent = 5
MAX(RL): visit 8 → v = 8, α = 8.
  8 ≥ β(+∞)? No.
  visit 1 → v = max(8,1) = 8.
  Returns 8.
MIN(R): v = 8, β = 8.

MAX(RR): visit 7 → v = 7, α = 5 (from root).
  visit 4 → v = max(7,4) = 7.
  Returns 7.
MIN(R): v = min(8,7) = 7. Returns 7.
MAX(root): max(5,7) = 7.

No leaves are pruned in this tree (no cutoff occurred
that prevented leaf evaluation). All 8 leaves evaluated.

Answer: No leaf nodes are pruned. This occurs because the move ordering is not favorable for pruning. If the tree had leaves in a different order, pruning would likely occur.

Question 7 GATE DA 2024

NAT: Leaf Evaluations with Alpha-Beta

Q (Numerical Answer Type): A game tree has uniform branching factor 2 and depth 4. In the best case, how many leaf nodes does alpha-beta pruning evaluate?

Solution

Total leaves without pruning: 24 = 16

Best-case formula for even depth: 2bd/2 − 1 = 2 × 22 − 1 = 2 × 4 − 1 = 7

Alternatively: b⌈d/2⌉ + b⌊d/2⌋ − 1 = 22 + 22 − 1 = 4 + 4 − 1 = 7

Answer: 7

Key Takeaways for GATE

  • Minimax backup: max at MAX nodes, min at MIN nodes, starting from leaves.
  • Alpha-beta: track α (MAX’s best) and β (MIN’s best); prune when α ≥ β.
  • Best-case alpha-beta: O(bd/2) — halves the exponent.
  • Expectiminimax: weighted average at CHANCE nodes.
  • Always trace left-to-right unless stated otherwise.
  • Count pruned nodes carefully — a pruned subtree includes all descendants.
