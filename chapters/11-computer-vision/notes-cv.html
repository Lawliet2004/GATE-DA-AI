VGG insight: Three 3×3 convs have same RF as one 7×7 but only 27C² vs 49C² params + 3 non-linearities.

ResNet insight: Skip connections create gradient highways; learning F(x) = 0 is easier than H(x) = x.

  • FCN: 1×1 conv replaces FC layers; variants FCN-32s, -16s, -8s

8. GATE DA Quick Tips

  • ⚡ Output size formula is the most testable — practise with varying padding, stride, and kernel sizes.
  • ⚡ Parameter counting: Remember Cout × (Cin × k² + 1). FC layers dominate total params in VGG/AlexNet.
  • ⚡ Receptive field: For all-stride-1, RF = 1 + ∑(kl − 1). Three 3×3 = RF of 7.
  • ⚡ ResNet skip connection: y = F(x) + x. Solves degradation in deep networks.
  • ⚡ IoU calculation: Draw the boxes, compute intersection dims carefully, then union = A + B − intersection.
  • ⚡ 1×1 convolutions are used for channel reduction (bottleneck), not spatial change.
  • ⚡ Depthwise separable conv saves ~k²× parameters compared to standard conv.
  • ⚡ Transposed conv output formula is the “reverse” of regular conv: (n−1)s − 2p + k.
  • ⚡ Semantic vs Instance: Semantic = per-pixel class only. Instance = per-object mask + class.
  • ⚡ mIoU > Pixel Accuracy for evaluation when classes are imbalanced.
