Chapter 0: Introduction — Notes & Cheatsheet

Revision Sheet

1. Key Definitions

  1. Artificial Intelligence (AI): The science and engineering of making intelligent machines that can perform tasks requiring human intelligence. Encompasses search, logic, planning, learning, perception, and language.
  2. Machine Learning (ML): Subset of AI. Systems that learn from data without being explicitly programmed. Defined by Mitchell: improves on task T with experience E as measured by P.
  3. Deep Learning (DL): Subset of ML. Neural networks with multiple hidden layers that automatically learn hierarchical feature representations from data.
  4. Rational Agent: An agent that acts to achieve the best expected outcome given its knowledge. Dominant modern AI approach (Russell & Norvig).
  5. Turing Test (1950): A machine passes if a human interrogator cannot distinguish its text responses from a human’s. Tests “Acting Humanly.”
  6. Total Turing Test: Extends the Turing Test to include computer vision (perceiving objects) and robotics (manipulating objects).
  7. Narrow AI (ANI): AI designed for a specific task. All current AI is narrow AI.
  8. General AI (AGI): Hypothetical AI matching human cognitive ability across all domains. Does not exist.
  9. Super AI (ASI): Hypothetical AI surpassing human intelligence in every domain. Theoretical only.
  10. Weak AI: Philosophical position: AI simulates but does not truly understand.
  11. Strong AI: Philosophical position: AI can genuinely understand and possess consciousness.
  12. Chinese Room Argument (Searle, 1980): Symbol manipulation without understanding is not true intelligence. Argues against Strong AI.
  13. Expert System: AI program using if-then rules from domain experts. Knowledge base + inference engine.
  14. McCulloch-Pitts Neuron (1943): First mathematical model of artificial neuron. Binary threshold unit.
  15. Perceptron (Rosenblatt, 1958): Single-layer neural network. Can learn linearly separable functions only.

2. Four Definitions of AI (Russell & Norvig)

Human-Centered Rationalist
Thought Thinking Humanly — Cognitive modeling; replicate human thought process Thinking Rationally — Laws of thought; formal logic as foundation
Behavior Acting Humanly — Turing Test; behavior indistinguishable from human Acting Rationally — Rational agent; maximize expected performance ✔

✔ Modern AI standard: Acting Rationally (Rational Agent approach)

4. AI Classification Quick Reference

By Capability

  • ANI (Narrow): Single task. Exists today. Examples: Siri, AlphaGo, GPT.
  • AGI (General): All human tasks. Does not exist.
  • ASI (Super): Surpasses all human ability. Theoretical.

By Functionality (Hintze)

  • Type I — Reactive: No memory, current input only. Example: Deep Blue.
  • Type II — Limited Memory: Uses recent data. Example: Self-driving cars, ChatGPT.
  • Type III — Theory of Mind: Models others’ mental states. Does not exist.
  • Type IV — Self-Aware: Possesses consciousness. Does not exist.

6. GATE DA Quick Facts

⚡ Exam Facts

  • Duration: 3 hours | Questions: 65 | Marks: 100
  • Types: MCQ, MSQ, NAT
  • Negative marking: MCQ only (−1/3 for 1-mark, −2/3 for 2-mark)
  • MSQ/NAT: No negative marking — always attempt!
  • AI section: ~15 marks, ~8–10 questions
  • High-priority AI topics: Search (BFS, DFS, A*, Minimax, Alpha-Beta), Logic (Propositional, Predicate), Probability (Bayes)
  • Primary textbook: Russell & Norvig — AIMA (4th ed.)
  • Time per question: ~2.77 minutes

8. Common GATE Traps to Avoid

  • ❌ AI = ML = DL → ✅ They are nested: DL ⊂ ML ⊂ AI
  • ❌ Turing Test = Definition of AI → ✅ It’s one approach (“Acting Humanly”); modern standard is Rational Agent
  • ❌ GPT is AGI → ✅ GPT is versatile Narrow AI (ANI), not AGI
  • ❌ Weak AI = Narrow AI → ✅ Weak/Strong is philosophical (understanding); Narrow/General is capability-based
  • ❌ Deep Blue learned from games → ✅ Deep Blue was reactive (Type I), no learning ability
  • ❌ Rational = Omniscient → ✅ Rational = best action given available info; Omniscient = knows everything
  • ❌ Chinese Room = Turing Test → ✅ They are different: Turing Test tests acting humanly; Chinese Room argues against Strong AI
