ℹ️ About This Course

Everything you need to know about how this platform works, its features, and how to get the most out of it for your GATE DA AI preparation.

📚 What This Website Covers

Master AI for GATE DA is a comprehensive, self-contained learning platform covering the entire Artificial Intelligence syllabus for the GATE Data Science & AI (DA) exam. The content spans 17 chapters and 118 topics, organized from foundational concepts to advanced applications.

Core AI Topics Covered:

  • Intelligent Agents — Agent architectures, rationality, PEAS framework
  • Search Algorithms — Uninformed (BFS, DFS, UCS, IDDFS) and Informed (A*, IDA*, Hill Climbing, Simulated Annealing, Genetic Algorithms)
  • Adversarial Search — Game trees, Minimax, Alpha-Beta Pruning, Monte Carlo Tree Search
  • Logic & Reasoning — Propositional logic, predicate logic (FOL), resolution, unification, skolemization
  • Probabilistic Reasoning — Bayesian networks, exact and approximate inference, HMMs, MCMC methods
  • Machine Learning Foundations — Bias-variance tradeoff, cross-validation, evaluation metrics
  • Neural Networks & Deep Learning — Perceptrons to Transformers, backpropagation, CNNs, RNNs, Attention
  • Applications — NLP (embeddings, LLMs, BERT, GPT), Computer Vision (object detection, segmentation), Reinforcement Learning (MDPs, Q-learning)
  • Planning & CSPs — STRIPS, partial-order planning, GraphPlan, arc consistency, constraint propagation
  • Advanced Topics — Explainable AI, AI ethics, federated learning, quantum ML, AI safety
  • GATE DA Preparation — Exam strategy, previous year questions, formula sheet, revision checklist

🎓 How to Use for GATE Preparation

  1. Start with the Roadmap. Visit the Learning Roadmap to understand the recommended study sequence. Follow the 7-phase plan from foundations to exam prep.
  2. Check the Syllabus Mapping. The Syllabus page maps every GATE DA AI topic to specific pages in the course. Use it to verify your coverage is complete.
  3. Focus on High-Priority Topics First. Chapters 2–7 (Search, Logic, Reasoning) carry the most marks. Ensure these are rock-solid before moving to applications.
  4. Use the Theory → Examples → Practice → Quiz Flow. For each chapter, read the theory pages, study the worked examples, try the interactive demos, then test yourself with the quiz.
  5. Practice Previous Year Questions. After completing each chapter, attempt the relevant previous year GATE questions in Chapter 16. This builds exam-specific problem-solving speed.
  6. Bookmark Difficult Topics. Click the bookmark button on pages you find challenging. Return to these during your revision phase.
  7. Use the Formula Sheet for Revision. The Formula Sheet in Chapter 16 compiles all key formulas. Review it weekly during your preparation.
  8. Track Your Progress. The homepage dashboard shows your completion percentage. Aim for 100% coverage of high-priority chapters before the exam.
  9. Final Revision with the Checklist. Two weeks before the exam, use the Final Revision Checklist to ensure nothing is missed.
  10. Avoid Common Mistakes. Read the Common Mistakes page to learn from errors that cost marks in GATE exams.
